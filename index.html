Shape Bias & Robustness Evaluation

via Cue Decomposition for Image Classification & Segmentation

Edgar Heinert • Thomas Gottwald • Annika Mütze • Matthias Rottmann

Download PDF

Abstract

We introduce a two‑step cue‑decomposition pipeline—Edge‑Enhancing Diffusion (EED) to isolate shape information and Voronoi‑based abstraction to capture texture—that lets us quantify how much deep nets rely on each cue. Building on this decomposition, we propose: (1) a new shape‑bias metric for both classifiers & segmenters, and (2) a robustness metric that out‑predicts prior approaches when models face corruptions from ImageNet‑C and Cityscapes‑C.

Key Contributions

Cue‑Decomposition Pipeline

Edge‑Enhancing Diffusion + Voronoi abstraction cleanly isolate shape vs. texture.

Universal Shape‑Bias Metric

Works for both classifiers and high‑res semantic segmentation models.

Improved Robustness Predictor

Correlates stronger (ρ > 0.9) with real performance under corruptions.

First Segmentation Bias Study

Analyzes Cityscapes & ADE20k—filling a gap left by prior shape‑bias work.
